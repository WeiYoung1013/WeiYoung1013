Multimodal Representations for Robot Learning

Why robot policies need representations that connect visual evidence, language, proprioception, and action.

Representation Should Serve Action

For robot learning, a representation is only useful if it preserves the details that affect the next action. A beautiful visual embedding can still fail if it ignores contact, occlusion, or the object's pose relative to the robot.

Modalities I Care About

  • Vision gives object, geometry, and scene context.
  • Language gives task intent and compositional structure.
  • Proprioception gives the robot its current physical state.
  • Touch and force signals reveal contact events that cameras often miss.

A Practical Test

I like probing whether a representation can predict near-future interaction outcomes: slip, collision, grasp stability, or whether a planned motion will enter a blocked region. These tests are closer to policy quality than generic reconstruction losses.

A robot representation should be multimodal, but it should also be action-aware.
