Humanoid Sim2Real Notes: What I Track First

A compact checklist for identifying the most expensive gaps between clean simulation and real humanoid behavior.

Signals Before Success Rate

Task success is useful, but it hides why a policy works. For humanoid sim-to-real transfer, I want the smaller signals first: contact timing, foot slip, recovery margin, torque saturation, sensor delay sensitivity, and falls by phase.

Checklist

  • Randomize terrain, mass, friction, and sensor noise separately before combining them.
  • Measure gait stability after disturbance, not only during nominal walking.
  • Log action smoothness and actuator saturation at the same frequency as control.
  • Keep a small set of replayable failure scenarios for every policy revision.

Debugging Pattern

When a real-world trial fails, I try to classify it as a state-estimation issue, a dynamics mismatch, a policy overfit, or an actuator limitation. That classification decides whether the next experiment should change training data, randomization, policy architecture, or hardware assumptions.
